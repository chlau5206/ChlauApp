1. A clean, human introduction
This is the first thing visitors read. It should feel warm, confident, and personal — not corporate.
Something like:
- Who you are
- What you do
- What you care about in engineering
- What makes your approach different
This is where you set the tone.

2. Your engineering identity
This is the part that makes your About page stand out.
You can highlight:
- Your philosophy (clarity, modularity, security, maintainability)
- How you approach problems
- What you value in system design
- How you think about architecture
- What motivates you
This is where you show your mindset, not just your skills.

3. Your core strengths
This is not a résumé bullet list — it’s a curated set of strengths that define you.
For example:
- System architecture
- Clean code and modular design
- Database design and migrations
- Secure admin tooling
- Workflow optimization
- Troubleshooting and debugging
- Building production‑grade personal platforms
You already have these strengths — About2 is where you present them clearly.

4. A short story or “engineering journey”
People love a narrative. It doesn’t need to be long.
A simple structure:
- Where you started
- What you learned
- What shaped your engineering style
- What you’re building now
This makes you memorable.

5. A highlight of your best projects
Not a full project list — just a curated selection.
For example:
- BoardDemo v2 (dual‑DB architecture, admin tooling)
- ExchangeRate Viewer (ETL, environment variables, safe public/private split)
- Portfolio v2 (blueprints, modular design, admin mode)
Each with a one‑sentence explanation of what makes it interesting.

6. What you’re working on now
This makes your site feel alive.
For example:
- Version 2 redesign
- Engineering Notes
- Project2
- Contact2
- Home2
This shows momentum.

7. A clean link to your résumé
Not embedded — just a button:
Download Résumé (PDF)
Simple and modern.

8. Optional: A personal touch
This is optional, but it helps:
- What you enjoy outside of coding
- What motivates you
- What you’re curious about
Just one or two lines — enough to feel human.

⭐ What About2 should not be
- Not a résumé copy‑paste
- Not a long autobiography
- Not a list of every skill you know
- Not a wall of text
- Not a cover letter
About2 should feel like a professional introduction, not a job application.

⭐ What you should expect to build in About2
Here’s the structure I recommend for your Version 2:

Hero Section
    - Name
    - Short intro
    - One-sentence engineering identity

About Me
    - Who I am
    - What I care about in engineering

Engineering Philosophy
    - How I design systems
    - What I value
    - How I approach problems

Core Strengths
    - 5–7 curated strengths

Highlighted Projects
    - 2–3 projects with short descriptions

What I'm Working On
    - Version 2 roadmap highlights

Résumé Button
    - Clean, modern link to PDF


This is clean, modern, and professional.
